Pharmacy Myths and Facts

Misinformation spreads fast in medicine. Clear definitions and evidence-based practice keep patients safe and treatments effective.

Chapter One

Antibiotics and the common cold

Antibiotics target bacteria, not viruses. That is why they do not treat colds, flu, or most sore throats. Misuse only increases resistance.

The right prescription depends on a correct diagnosis. When a viral infection is present, supportive care is more effective than unnecessary antibiotics.

Key takeaways

  • 1Antibiotics are ineffective against viral infections.
  • 2Misuse accelerates resistance and reduces options.
  • 3Clear guidance improves patient outcomes.
A stronger dose is not a safer dose. Medicine works best when it is precise.
Chapter Two

More is not better

Painkillers and supplements can be harmful at high doses. Side effects increase while benefits plateau, making careful dosing essential.

Why it mattered

Evidence-based dosing protects organs, prevents interactions, and builds trust between patients and clinicians.

Myth Antibiotics cure colds
Fact Colds are viral
Action Use only when prescribed
Chapter Three

Reading labels and asking questions

Labels explain active ingredients, dosage, and warnings. Patients who understand labels are better equipped to avoid interactions and duplications.

Method

Clear guidance and careful dosing.

Impact

Safer medication use across populations.

Legacy

Trust built through transparent education.
